About

The Poolesville Math Tournament (PVMT) is ran by dedicated Poolesville High School students from the Poolesville Math Team. We invite middle school students of all backgrounds and skill levels to participate!

The contest will have two divisions, 1 and 2, so that competitors' skill levels are well matched. Division 2 is meant for students who are relatively new to competition math and don't have much experience with the Mathcounts/AMC competitions. Division 1 is meant for those who are familiar with contest math, and will thus have harder problems. Please choose your division appropriately based on your skill level; it is highly discouraged for experienced students to purposely choose Division 2. Note that all members on a team must be in the same division.

Students will compete in teams of at most 4 people. Smaller teams (of 1, 2, and 3 people) are also allowed, but note that these teams will be at a disadvantage for the team rounds because they have fewer people. There is no limit to how many teams a particular school/organization may register.


Format

Competitors from both divisions will take two subject tests, an individual round, a team round, and a gust round. The four subject tests available are Algebra, Geometry, Number Theory, and Combinatorics, and each subject test will be 45 minutes and consist of 8 problems. The individual round is 60 minutes and will consist of 15 problems; the team round is 45 minutes and consists of 20 problems.


Schedule

The current tentative date for the competition is May 21, 2022. The date and schedule have not been finalized yet, and more information will be released closer to the date of the competition.
